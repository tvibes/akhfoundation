We know that health is the greatest gift

The benefit of good health can never be overstressed, and we desire to make it accessible to all. Therefore, the Foundation is intensely focused on providing specialized medical attention in the Niger Delta region. This will be in the areas of communicable diseases and other ailments rampant in the Niger Delta region such as guinea worm, tuberculosis, etc.

The key to cost-effective health care management is prevention, which can only be accomplished through health care education. In rural areas, diseases are rampant due to lack of hygiene and proper education or understanding of common diseases. A.K. Horsfall Foundation will develop simple and easy-to-understand informational brochures on common diseases such as guinea worm, tuberculosis, etc.

Our Projects

While health care is always in huge demand almost in every society, access to such is very limited to remote dwellers due to terrain and economic condition, thus, resulting in avoidable loss of lives to diseases and ailments Due to this risk, the A.K Horsfall Foundation has set sight extending quality health care services to those in remote communities by sponsoring specialized medical programs in the Niger Delta. This program covers different areas in health, and is made to benefit the people directly.

  • Educative

    A.K. Horsfall Foundation will develop simple and easy-to-understand informational brochures on common diseases such as guinea worm, tuberculosis, etc.

    Paris, France

  • Medicare

    We sponsor healthcare programs in remote areas (other than the catchment area) and arranges for medical teams, medical supplies and medicines to benefit the poor.

    London, England

  • Medical Research

    The Foundation to offer support grant to qualified and competent Post Graduate Research Students in areas of ailments and diseases endemic in the riverine areas.

    Venice, Italy

  • Rewards

    We encourage academic excellence in the faculties of medicine of Universities in catchments areas by supporting the endowment of professorial chairs, and students.

    Sydney, Australia
